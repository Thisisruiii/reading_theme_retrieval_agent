Title: A Room of One's Own
Author: Virginia Woolf
Themes: money and independence, education, writing and creativity, freedom, gender norms
Notes: Woolf argues that a woman needs money, privacy, and education in order to write. The note is useful for questions about creative work, intellectual freedom, and the material conditions behind literature.

Title: Kim Ji-young, Born 1982
Author: Cho Nam-joo
Themes: motherhood, marriage, work and career, family, social expectations, gender norms, identity
Notes: The novel follows Kim Ji-young through school, work, marriage, and motherhood. It shows how ordinary social expectations can limit a woman's choices and sense of self.

Title: The Handmaid's Tale
Author: Margaret Atwood
Themes: violence, survival, freedom, motherhood, identity, social expectations, gender norms
Notes: The story imagines a society where women's bodies and identities are controlled by law and ritual. It is useful for discussing survival, loss of freedom, and forced motherhood.

Title: Concerning My Daughter
Author: Kim Hye-jin
Themes: mother-daughter relationship, family, identity, social expectations, healing
Notes: A mother struggles to understand her adult daughter and her daughter's partner. The book is useful for questions about family conflict, care, acceptance, and changing social values.

Title: Bright Night
Author: Choi Eun-young
Themes: family, memory, mother-daughter relationship, healing, survival, sisterhood
Notes: The novel connects several generations of women through remembered pain and care. It is strong for questions about family memory, inherited grief, and healing through storytelling.

Title: Little Women
Author: Louisa May Alcott
Themes: sisterhood, family, girlhood, writing and creativity, marriage, work and career
Notes: The March sisters grow up with different dreams, talents, and ideas about love. The notes are useful for questions about sisterhood, girlhood, creative ambition, and family duty.

Title: My Brilliant Friend
Author: Elena Ferrante
Themes: friendship, girlhood, education, family, social expectations, money and independence, identity, violence
Notes: This novel follows the intense friendship between two girls growing up in a poor neighborhood in Naples. It is useful for questions about female friendship, girlhood, education, class pressure, family expectations, and the struggle for independence.

Title: Misogyny
Author: Chizuko Ueno
Themes: gender norms, violence, social expectations, identity, education
Notes: This nonfiction text examines how misogyny can appear in culture, language, institutions, and everyday habits. It helps answer conceptual questions about gender norms and social structures.

Title: Cat's Eye
Author: Margaret Atwood
Themes: girlhood, friendship, memory, violence, identity, healing
Notes: Elaine remembers painful childhood friendships and the long effects of cruelty. The book is useful for questions about girlhood, memory, emotional violence, and the difficulty of healing.

Title: The Color Purple
Author: Alice Walker
Themes: sisterhood, violence, healing, freedom, identity, survival, writing and creativity
Notes: Celie survives abuse and gradually finds her voice through letters, relationships, and self-discovery. This note is useful for questions about survival, sisterhood, healing, and writing as self-expression.
