friendship: Relationships between friends, including support, jealousy, trust, conflict, and emotional growth.

sisterhood: Bonds between sisters or women who support each other like sisters. This theme often includes solidarity, care, and shared survival.

family: Relationships inside families, including duty, conflict, love, pressure, and responsibility.

mother-daughter relationship: The emotional and social relationship between mothers and daughters, including misunderstanding, care, inheritance, and reconciliation.

marriage: Stories about married life, romantic partnership, domestic labor, social duty, and the limits or support created by marriage.

motherhood: Experiences of being a mother or being shaped by ideas about mothers, including care, sacrifice, pressure, and identity.

work and career: Jobs, professional ambition, workplace inequality, labor, and the conflict between paid work and private life.

money and independence: How economic resources affect freedom, safety, education, and the ability to make choices.

education: Learning, school, intellectual growth, and access to knowledge.

writing and creativity: Creative expression, authorship, storytelling, letters, art, and the need for space to create.

girlhood: Childhood and teenage experiences, especially how girls learn social rules, friendship, shame, ambition, or fear.

memory: Remembering the past, family history, trauma, nostalgia, and how memories shape identity.

violence: Physical, emotional, social, or institutional harm. This theme should be handled carefully and based on retrieved notes.

healing: Recovery after pain, conflict, or trauma. Healing can come through relationships, memory, creativity, or independence.

freedom: The ability to choose one's life, body, work, relationships, and identity.

social expectations: Rules that society places on people, such as how women should behave, marry, work, care, or remain silent.

gender norms: Ideas about what women and men are expected to be. This theme is useful for comparing books that question those expectations.

identity: A person's sense of self, including name, voice, role, body, family background, and social position.

survival: Continuing to live and protect the self under difficult conditions, including violence, poverty, isolation, or social control.
